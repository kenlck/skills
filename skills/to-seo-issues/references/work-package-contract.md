# Work-package contract

A work package is the smallest bounded unit one executor can complete and verify without inventing inputs, obtaining unstated authority, or waiting for an organic outcome.

## Mandatory split boundaries

Split whenever the primary deliverable, required capability or owner, access or approval boundary, dependency state, verification method, or measurement window changes. Do not bundle research, production, approval, publication, distribution, and outcome review merely because they serve one initiative.

Every package states its outcome and rationale, audience and intent when relevant, deliverable, scope and non-goals, inputs and authority, delivery acceptance, completion evidence, dependencies, fallback, and measurement contribution.

## Archetypes

Give each package exactly one primary archetype.

| Archetype | Controllable completion evidence |
|---|---|
| Baseline/setup | Required access, inventory, instrumentation, or baseline exists and is verified. |
| Evidence/brief | A decision-ready brief identifies sources, assumptions, intent, constraints, and the next executor's specification. |
| Production | The bounded content, schema, citation data, optimization asset, or other deliverable satisfies its stated rubric. |
| Publication/change | An approved change is applied to the named surface and verified through that surface's observable interface. |
| Outreach cycle | An approved, finite prospect batch is contacted and the activity log is complete; replies, placements, and links are outcomes. |
| Outcome review | Evidence for the defined window is collected and a continue, adjust, or stop decision is recorded. |
| Engineering handoff | The SEO behavior, constraints, acceptance evidence, and dependency context are sufficient for `/to-issues` to decompose the code work. |

Quality assurance belongs inside delivery acceptance unless a different owner, authority, environment, or verification method makes it a separate package.

## Dependency classes

- **Hard prerequisite** — without it, the downstream deliverable cannot exist or would be invalid. Use sparingly.
- **Parallel external bet** — a third-party response, earned placement, paid access, or external approval may improve results but does not block controllable work.
- **Enhancement** — useful but optional; proceed without it.

Every external bet names the controllable deliverable, the uncontrolled result, a deadline or review point, a no-response or no-budget fallback, and the measurement assumption that changes if it fails. External or paid work may be a hard prerequisite only when no valid downstream deliverable exists without it. Failure of a genuinely essential external prerequisite triggers an initiative or strategy review instead of an indefinite blocked chain.

## Readiness

Apply `ready-for-seo-agent` only when all of these are true:

- one primary archetype and bounded deliverable;
- every hard prerequisite is complete;
- required inputs, access, authority, and approvals are present;
- delivery acceptance is observable and within the executor's control;
- no human or engineering coordination is hidden inside the package;
- for an outcome review, the observation window has elapsed.

Remove or withhold the label when any condition fails. Never use delayed rankings, traffic, citations, replies, or conversions as delivery acceptance.

## Required labels

Create a missing label only when its creation was included in the approved mutation set.

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `ready-for-seo-agent` | The package passes every readiness condition and can be executed now by the SEO issue executor. |
| `seo-outcome-review` | The issue evaluates delayed evidence and records a continue, adjust, or stop decision. It may not be ready yet. |
| `seo-strategy` | The issue is the durable tracker snapshot and parent for one approved strategy thesis. It is not executable. |
| `seo-engineering-handoff` | The issue must be decomposed through the displayed `/to-issues` command. It is not executable by the SEO issue executor. |

## Mixed initiatives

Keep editorial, measurement, publication, outreach, local, and review packages in this graph. Represent code-heavy work as an Engineering handoff labeled `seo-engineering-handoff`; it is not ready for the SEO executor. Its body and the final summary must show `/to-issues <issue-url>`. The resulting engineering slices link back to that handoff, while parallel SEO packages continue.

## Outcome reviews and recurring work

Create one outcome review per initiative or experiment. Combine reviews only when hypothesis, baseline, indicators, guardrails, and observation window are identical. Publish the review with the delivery graph, label it `seo-outcome-review`, and withhold readiness until dependencies and time gates clear.

Represent recurring work as a finite batch or timebox with a concrete output, completion evidence, review point, and renew, adjust, automate, or stop decision. Never create an open-ended ongoing package.
