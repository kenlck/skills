# Opportunity framework

## Thesis

A thesis is a falsifiable claim that, for a defined audience and market, selected SEO/GEO activities are the best organic-growth direction for a stated business outcome. It must explain why this direction wins now, what would disprove it, and why the alternatives lost.

## Opportunity portfolio

Evaluate credible opportunities with `High`, `Medium`, `Low`, or `Unknown` plus a short rationale. Never calculate an aggregate numeric score.

| Criterion | Question |
|---|---|
| Business value | Could this contribute to the valuable conversion or a necessary precursor? |
| Audience and intent fit | Does it reach the right people in a useful decision state? |
| Evidence strength | How direct, current, and complete is the supporting evidence? |
| Competitive attainability | Can this site plausibly earn visibility against the observed field? |
| Site and authority fit | Does the site possess or have a credible path to the required depth, proof, and authority? |
| Execution complexity | Which capabilities, dependencies, and off-site effort does it require? |
| Time to signal | When should a useful leading signal become observable? |
| Durability | Will the value persist beyond a short-lived query or platform behavior? |
| SEO potential | What search discovery, ranking, linking, or conversion path can it create? |
| GEO potential | What entity, evidence, citation, retrieval, or source-worthiness advantage can it create? |

Choose one primary bet and one secondary or contingent bet. Tie each bet to a defined market and language. A secondary bet is not a second priority-one initiative: state the trigger that would activate it. Keep capacity resource-neutral when the user has not supplied constraints; use qualitative effort and required capabilities, never fabricated hours.

## Measurement contract

Each bet requires:

- primary business outcome
- leading indicators
- guardrails against irrelevant traffic, cannibalization, or conversion harm
- baseline status: known, unknown, or must be established
- review window
- continue, adjust, and stop rules

Draft this contract for the user. Missing analytics does not block the thesis; establishing the baseline becomes a roadmap initiative. Require the contract for the two bets, not every minor action.

## Planning horizon

- **Six months:** strategic direction and expected outcome window.
- **Ninety days:** execution-ready initiatives for the active bet.
- **Thirty-day checkpoints:** evidence and delivery reviews, not forced strategy changes.
- **Four to eight weeks for GEO experiments:** review observable signals while acknowledging that authority and third-party citations may take longer.

Review earlier when the business goal changes, a decision threshold is reached, material results contradict the thesis, a competitor or opportunity changes the field, or a dependency invalidates the roadmap. Do not pivot on brief ranking volatility.

## Approval and lifecycle

The current strategy is `Proposed` or `Active`; archived strategies are `Superseded`.

- With no current strategy, an unapproved thesis may be written to `SEO-STRATEGY.md` as Proposed.
- With an Active strategy, place an unapproved replacement in `seo-strategy/proposals/`; never overwrite or downgrade the Active strategy.
- On approval, archive the prior Active strategy before replacement, then mark the selected proposal Promoted.
- On rejection or deferral, leave the Active strategy unchanged and preserve the evidence.

Archive before changing the business objective, primary conversion, audience, market, language, thesis, primary or secondary bet, six-month direction, measurement outcome, or decision threshold. Do not archive wording fixes, progress updates, supporting links, within-thesis sequencing, review-date changes, or factual corrections that leave direction intact.

## Proposal lifecycle

- **Captured:** recorded without research.
- **Validated:** evidence supports retaining it as a candidate.
- **Deferred:** viable, but its revisit date or trigger has not arrived.
- **Rejected:** evidence argues against pursuing it.
- **Promoted:** approved into the active strategy.

Proposal status changes must record the date, reason, and supporting research. Proposal files are mutable; research and history files are immutable. A promoted proposal links to the Active strategy, and the Active strategy links back.
