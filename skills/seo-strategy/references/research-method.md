# Research method

Build evidence for a decision, not a generic SEO checklist. Record the research date, primary market, language, queries or prompts used, and source links. Search results vary by location, time, and personalization; report observations, never universal rankings.

## Infer first

Inspect, when present:

- `SEO-STRATEGY.md` and `seo-strategy/`
- `SEO.md` and the latest `seo-reports/`
- product or marketing context files
- routes, content, metadata sources, and site configuration
- configured deployed URL and the live site

Infer the business model, valuable conversion, audience, market, and language from evidence. Present material inferences plainly; the frame check in the skill is where the user confirms or corrects them. An audit report is evidence, not a prerequisite; recommend `/seo` as a roadmap dependency only when a technical unknown could invalidate the thesis.

## Research frontier by branch

### Validate

Research the supplied keyword, topic, or activity; its intent and competing result types; adjacent alternatives; existing site overlap; and any obvious technical, architectural, measurement, or authority prerequisite that could deserve priority instead.

### Discover

Survey plausible directions across content and keyword targeting, existing-content improvement, architecture and internal linking, technical foundations, original research, digital PR and links, third-party citations and partnerships, local or programmatic opportunities when relevant, GEO source-worthiness, and measurement foundations.

### Compare

Test every named alternative on equal evidence. Add one credible challenger when the evidence exposes a materially better direction; do not add a token alternative merely to fill the slot.

### Refresh

Test the active thesis against observed results, current market conditions, material business changes, and eligible proposals. Inventory `Captured`, `Validated`, and `Deferred` proposals. Reconsider Deferred items only when their revisit trigger or date has arrived; screen Captured items before deeper research; treat Validated items as candidates. Ignore Rejected and Promoted items unless the user asks.

## Evidence lanes

Cover the lanes that can change the decision:

- **Demand and intent:** observable query language, result types, funnel stage, audience problem, and conversion fit.
- **Competitive landscape:** search competitors, business competitors, dominant formats, authority patterns, saturation, and gaps.
- **Site fit:** existing pages, topical coverage, cannibalization, internal paths, proprietary experience or data, and technical constraints visible without a full audit.
- **Off-site authority:** credible publications, directories, communities, partners, citations, link pathways, and promotion requirements.
- **GEO:** entity clarity, factual density, original evidence, quotability, machine-readable structure, source credibility, and presence in third-party sources likely to inform answer engines.
- **Performance evidence:** Search Console, analytics, rank tracking, backlink tools, or user-provided exports when available.

## Evidence discipline

- Prefer current primary or first-party evidence for claims about platforms, the business, and performance.
- Use live market and SERP observations for external competition; link and date them.
- Remain tool-agnostic. Incorporate Search Console, analytics, Ahrefs, Semrush, Moz, or similar data only when available, and name the source and date.
- Never invent search volume, keyword difficulty, traffic, conversion, backlink, citation, or AI-visibility data.
- Separate observation, inference, and hypothesis. Lower confidence when evidence is indirect or incomplete.
- Do not promise rankings or visibility in an AI product. Recommend a measurable experiment when attribution is weak.
- For an offline preliminary pass, label external conclusions unverified and put live validation before commitment in the roadmap.

Research is complete only when another reader can trace every decision-changing claim to a source, observation, disclosed inference, or explicit unknown.
